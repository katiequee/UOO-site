FAQ

I only play an unconventional instrument/would like to play an unconventional instrument. Will I still be able to participate in the orchestra?

In short, yes, however there are conditions that will be listed below. We want as many people to be able to play with us as possible! However, we’ve run into this situation many times where someone’s instrument simple does not fit the song, and we figure it is best to lay down some guidelines as to when these instruments will be able to be used:

  • Any Ocarina - You may not be able to play in most songs because the tone of a ocarina simply does not fit the song. Arrangers will not be prevented from adding ocarina parts if they wish, but they may not double up on another part if one is not there.
  • Acoustic/Electric/Classical Guitar - You may not be able to play in most songs because the tone of a guitar simply does not fit the song. However, there will be songs in the future that will include many guitars such as Hopes and Dreams and Death by Glamour. This guideline does not include Bass Guitar, which may double up on Contrabass parts. If you do submit a Bass Guitar part, please use a clean amp setting.
  • Accordion- You may not be able to play in most songs because the tone of an accordion does not fit with many songs. However, there will be songs where the accordion does fit such as Nyeh Heh Heh! or Alphys.
  • Vocals - Will not be available on any full orchestra song. May be on very limited small ensembles, but it is not recommend to join if you are only a vocalist.

If you play an unconventional orchestra instrument that is not on this list and would like to know what our policy on it is, please ask!

What are typical orchestra instruments that will be included in every song?

Typical orchestra instruments that will always have a part are:

Woodwinds

  • Piccolo and Flute
  • Clarinet and Bass Clarinet
  • Alto, Tenor, and Baritone Saxophone
  • Bassoon
  • Oboe

Brass

  • Trumpet
  • French Horn
  • Trombone
  • Euphonium
  • Tuba

Strings

  • Violin
  • Viola
  • Cello
  • Contrabass

Keyboards

  • Piano

Percussion

  • Percussion will change drastically per song requirements, but percussionists can expect a typical line up of drums, pitched percussion, and auxiliary percussion as the piece requires.

I’m not receiving welcome emails and/or sheet music emails. What do I do?

Some of our members have reported welcome emails or sheet music emails sometimes getting sent to their spam folder. Please check there if you haven’t already!

Otherwise, we may have the incorrect email for you. Please contact us at undertune2k15@gmail.com so we can sort out this issue!

How/where do I submit my recordings?

You email your recordings to undertune2k15@gmail.com . If you submit your recordings through cloud based links like Google Drive or OneDrive then please make sure that you share your recording with our email so we can view it!
